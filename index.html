Chapter 01

The Electric Current

Ohm's law, resistance, EMF, series & parallel circuits

CORE DEFINITIONS
CH1 — Electric Current
I = Q/t = Ne/t
Flow of electric charge through a conductor. Conventional direction: + to − outside source. e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C.
Unit: Ampere (A) = C/s
CH1 — Potential Difference
V = W/Q
Work done to transfer 1 Coulomb between two points. Q = N×e.
Unit: Volt (V) = J/C
CH1 — Resistance
R = V/I = ρₑl/A
Opposition to current due to electron collisions. ρₑ = resistivity. Compare: R₁/R₂ = ρ₁l₁A₂/(ρ₂l₂A₁).
Unit: Ohm (Ω) = V/A
CH1 — Ohm's Law
V = IR
At constant temperature I ∝ V. Slope of I-V graph = 1/R. Slope of R-l graph = ρₑ/A.
Const T: I α V
CH1 — Power
P = IV = I²R = V²/R
Rate of electrical energy dissipation. Three equivalent forms from Ohm's law.
Unit: Watt (W) = J/s
CH1 — Energy
E = Pt = QV = I²Rt = V²t/R
Total energy consumed over time t. Also: E = W (Joule's Law). 1 kWh = 3.6×10⁶ J.
Unit: Joule (J)
CH1 — Conductivity
σ = 1/ρₑ = l/(RA)
Inverse of resistivity. Unit: Ω⁻¹·m⁻¹. Two wires same material → ρₑ constant.
Resistivity: ρₑ = RA/l
CH1 — Wire Reshaping
R₁/R₂ = l₁²/l₂² = r₂⁴/r₁⁴
Volume constant when wire stretched: V = Al = const. l₁A₁ = l₂A₂. Note: "to double" → ×2; "by double" → ×3.
Const volume ratio
RESISTOR CONNECTIONS

⛓ Series Connection

• All R carry same I
• V divides: V ∝ R
• Rₑq = R₁+R₂+R₃
• Equal n: Rₑq = nR
• V₁/V₂ = R₁/R₂
• Vₜ = V₁ + V₂ + V₃

⑂ Parallel Connection

• All R have same V
• I divides: I ∝ 1/R
• 1/Rₑq = 1/R₁+1/R₂+1/R₃
• Two R: R₁R₂/(R₁+R₂)
• Equal n: Rₑq = R/n
• Iₜ = I₁ + I₂ + I₃

EMF & CLOSED CIRCUIT
CH1 — EMF Closed Circuit
I = Vᴮ/(Rₜ + r)
Total work done to transfer 1C inside AND outside source.
Discharge
V = Vᴮ − Ir
Charge
V = Vᴮ + Ir
CH1 — V-I Battery Graph
V = Vᴮ − I·r
Slope of V-I graph = −r (discharge). y-intercept = Vᴮ. x-intercept = Vᴮ/r (short circuit I).
Discharge slope
= −r
Charge slope
= +r
CH1 — 2 Batteries (Opposing)
I = (Vᴮ₁−Vᴮ₂)/(R+r₁+r₂)
Aiding batteries: I = (Vᴮ₁+Vᴮ₂)/(R+r₁+r₂). Bigger V = source (discharging).
KEY FORMULA
CH1 — Kirchhoff's Laws
ΣIin = ΣIout | ΣVB = ΣIR
1st Law: charge conservation at node. 2nd Law: energy conservation in loop. Also ΣV=0 (useful for finding potential of a point).
KIRCHHOFF
GRAPHS & RATIOS
📊 Important Graph Slopes
  • Slope of Q-t graph = I (current intensity)
  • Slope of I-V graph = 1/R (reciprocal of resistance)
  • Slope of R-l graph = ρₑ/A (for constant cross-section)
  • Slope of V-I battery = −r (internal resistance, negative for discharge)
  • Slope of φm-t graph = −emf/N (Faraday's law)
Chapter 02

Magnetic Effect of Electric Current

Magnetic flux, field due to current, forces, torque & measuring instruments

MAGNETIC FLUX & FIELD
CH2 — Magnetic Flux
φₘ = B·A·cosθ
θ = angle between normal to coil and B. Coil ∥ B → φ=0. Coil ⊥ B → φ=max. If coil perpendicular then rotates x°: θ=x. If parallel then rotates x°: θ=90−x.
Unit: Weber (Wb) = T·m²
CH2 — Straight Wire B
B = μI/(2πd)
Ampere Circuital Law. At distance d from axis. Neutral point: I₁/d = I₂/(x−d) same dir; I₁/d = I₂/(x+d) opposite dir.
μ₀ = 4π×10⁻⁷ T·m/A
CH2 — Circular Coil Centre
B = μIN/(2r)
At centre. Ring N=1. Part of loop: N=θ/360°. Wire recoiled: N₁/N₂ = r₂/r₁. Wire tangent to coil: d = r.
At centre of coil
CH2 — Solenoid
B = μIN/l = μIn
At mid-axis. n = N/l = turns/m (constant even when cut). When turns touching: l = N·2r_wire. B_circular/B_solenoid = l/(2r).
n = turns per meter
CH2 — Resultant B
Bₜ = √(B₁² + B₂²) when ⊥
Same dir: add. Opposite: subtract (large−small). Perpendicular: vector sum. Neutral point in subtraction region near lower current wire.
SUPERPOSITION
CH2 — Two Coils
Same plane + same dir → B₁+B₂
Same plane + opposite dir → B₁−B₂. Perpendicular planes → √(B₁²+B₂²). Common axis same/opposite: add or subtract.
MAGNETIC FORCE & TORQUE
CH2 — Force on Wire
F = BIl·sinθ
θ = angle between wire and B (not normal). Wire ∥ B → F=0. Wire ⊥ B → F=max. Direction: Fleming's Left-Hand Rule. Weight of wire = F in equilibrium.
Wire ⊥ B
F = BIl (max)
Wire ∥ B
F = 0
CH2 — Force 2 Wires
F = μI₁I₂l/(2πd)
Same direction → Attraction. Opposite direction → Repulsion. For 3 wires: use resultant B or find each pairwise force.
MUTUAL FORCE
CH2 — Magnetic Torque
τ = BIANsinθ
θ = angle between normal to coil and B. τ_max when coil ∥ to B (θ=90°). τ=0 when coil ⊥ to B (θ=0). Also τ = |md|·B·sinθ.
τ_max position
coil ∥ B
τ = 0 position
coil ⊥ B
Unit: N·m
CH2 — Magnetic Dipole
|m⃗d| = IAN
Does NOT depend on any angle. Constant during rotation. τ = |md|·B·sinθ.
Unit: A·m² = N·m/T
NO ANGLE DEP.
MEASURING INSTRUMENTS
INSTRUMENTS — Galvanometer
τ = BIANsinθ → θ=90° (radial)
Iron core ↑ field density. Concave poles → radial B → uniform scale. Spiral springs: lead I in/out, restore pointer. Sensitivity S = θ/I (°/A).
INSTRUMENTS — Ammeter
Rₛ = IgRg/(I−Ig)
Shunt Rₛ (small R) in parallel with G. Ig/I = Rₛ/(Rₛ+Rg). Increasing range → decreasing sensitivity.
Ig/I = Rₛ/(Rₛ+Rg)
PARALLEL — SMALL R
INSTRUMENTS — Voltmeter
Rₘ = (V−Vg)/Ig
Multiplier Rₘ (large R) in series. V = Ig(Rg+Rₘ). V₁/V₂ = (Rg+Rₘ₁)/(Rg+Rₘ₂).
SERIES — LARGE R
INSTRUMENTS — Ohmmeter
I = Vᴮ/(R₀+Rₓ)
Before Rₓ: Ig = Vᴮ/R₀ (full deflection). Scale non-uniform (reversed). Mid-scale when Rₓ=R₀. R₀ = Rg+Rc+Rv+r.
Ig/I = (R₀+Rₓ)/R₀
Chapter 03

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday, Lenz, generators, transformers & DC motors

FARADAY & LENZ
CH3 — Faraday's Law
emf = −NΔφₘ/Δt
Average induced EMF. Slope φm-t = −emf/N. Q = NΔφm/R. ΔB=B when coil ∥→⊥. ΔB=2B when coil turned from ⊥.
Avg P.D.
= QR/t
Charge Q
= NΔφm/R
FARADAY'S LAW
CH3 — Lenz's Rule
Induced I opposes Δφₘ
N approaching → Inc φ → coil repels (induced S faces magnet N) → counter-clockwise. N receding → Dec φ → coil attracts → clockwise. Used for coil (not straight wire).
CH3 — Straight Wire emf
emf = B·l·v·sinθ
θ = angle between wire/velocity and B. Wire must cut M.F. lines. v in km/hr → multiply by 5/18. Direction: Fleming's Right-Hand Rule.
Wire ⊥ B
emf = Blv
Wire ∥ B
emf = 0
CH3 — Mutual Induction
emfₛ = −M·ΔIₚ/Δt
emf in secondary from changing primary current. M = μANₛNₚ/lₚ. MΔIₚ = NₛΔφₘ.
Unit: Henry (H)
CH3 — Self Induction
emf = −L·ΔI/Δt
L = μAN²/l. LΔI = NΔφ. At closing: I=0, back emf=max=Vᴮ. At 75% max I → back emf = 25% Vᴮ. At opening: large spark.
Close switch
I=0, emf=max
I=max
emf=0
CH3 — Eddy Currents
Induced in metallic cores
Disadvantage: Energy loss as heat.
Use: Inductive furnace (melting metals).
Reduce: Laminated core (thin insulated sheets ∥ to B).
ENERGY LOSS
AC GENERATOR (DYNAMO)
CH3 — emf Instantaneous
emf = NBAω·sinθ = emf_max·sinθ
θ from normal to B. ω = 2πf = 2π/T = v/r. If coil starts ⊥: θ=ωt. If starts ∥: θ=ωt±90°. Part of cycle: θ=part×360°.
emf_max
= NBAω
Coil ∥ B
emf = emf_max
CH3 — Effective (RMS)
emf_eff = emf_max/√2 ≈ 0.707×emf_max
Used in power calculations. P = I_eff·V_eff. Occurs when angle between coil/normal and B = 45°. Half-max emf: angle = 30°.
RMS VALUE
CH3 — Average emf
emf_av = 2emf_max/π (¼ cycle from ⊥)
For ½ cycle from ⊥: = 2emf_max/π. From ∥ for ½ cycle: = 0. ¾ cycle from ⊥: same as ¼/3. Complete cycle: 0. General: use Faraday's law with Δcosθ.
CH3 — AC Times at Max/Zero
Max reached: 2N times/cycle
Starting from zero position: max reached 2N times, zero reached 2N+1 times. Full wave rectifier (commutator) doubles frequency: 2f.
CH3 — AC Rectification Types
Full wave: commutator. Half wave: + diode
Slip rings → AC (f). Commutator (1 coil) → full-wave DC (2f). Multiple coils → smoother DC. Diode in circuit → half-wave DC (f).
TRANSFORMER
CH3 — Ideal Transformer
Vₚ/Vₛ = Nₚ/Nₛ = Iₛ/Iₚ
η = 100%. Pₚ = Pₛ. AC only (cannot step-up DC). Step Up: Nₛ>Nₚ. Step Down: Nₛ
Step Up
Nₛ > Nₚ
Step Down
Nₛ < Nₚ
CH3 — Non-Ideal Transformer
η = IₛVₛ×100/(IₚVₚ)
Losses: ① Wire resistance → heat (use copper). ② Eddy currents (use laminated Si-iron). ③ Mechanical vibration. ④ M.F. leakage. Open secondary → no energy consumed (back emf = source emf).
CH3 — Power Transmission
P_lost = I²R_wires
Step-up at station → ↑V → ↓I → ↓losses. R_wires = 2d×R_unit. V_end = V_start − IR_wires. η_trans = P_consumer/P_station × 100.
CH3 — DC Motor
M. torque on current coil in B field
E→KE conversion. Commutator (split cylinder) ensures unidirectional rotation. Inertia continues past τ=0. Back emf regulates speed: load↑→speed↓→back emf↓→I↑→torque↑.
Chapter 04

AC Circuits

Reactance, impedance, resonance & phase angles

REACTANCE & IMPEDANCE
CH4 — Inductive Reactance
X_L = ωL = 2πfL
Opposes AC current due to self-induction. X_L ∝ f (direct). Current through pure coil: I = NBA/L (independent of f!). Series: Lt = L₁+L₂. Parallel: 1/Lt = 1/L₁+1/L₂.
Unit: Ohm (Ω)
CH4 — Capacitive Reactance
X_C = 1/(2πfC) = 1/(ωC)
X_C ∝ 1/f (inverse). Current through capacitor I ∝ f². Blocks DC completely. Series: 1/Ct = 1/C₁+1/C₂. Parallel: Ct = C₁+C₂+... (opposite to R).
Unit: Ohm (Ω)
CH4 — Impedance
Z = V_total/I_total
R+L: Z = √(X_L²+R²). R+C: Z = √(X_C²+R²). L+C (pure): Z = X_L−X_C. R+L+C: Z = √(R²+(X_L−X_C)²).
Unit: Ohm (Ω)
CH4 — Voltage in AC
V_R+L = √(V_L²+V_R²)
R+C: √(V_C²+V_R²). Pure L+C: V = V_L−V_C. R+L+C: √(V_R²+(V_L−V_C)²). V_L leads I by 90°. V_C lags I by 90°.
CH4 — Phase Angle
tanθ = X_L/R or X_C/R
θ between total V and I. cosθ = R/Z (adjacent/hypotenuse). sinθ = X/Z. In AC: power = I_eff²×R (ohmic only consumes power).
CH4 — Resonance
f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)
When X_L = X_C: Z = R (minimum), I = max, phase angle = 0. V_L = V_C. Compare: f₁/f₂ = √(L₂C₂/L₁C₁). Used in radio tuning circuits.
RESONANCE
FREQUENCY EFFECTS
📊 How frequency f affects circuit quantities
  • Ohmic R: R unchanged with f. Current I ∝ f (because emf_max = NBAω ∝ f).
  • Pure Inductor: X_L ∝ f. But I = NBA/L → independent of f! (f cancels)
  • Capacitor: X_C ∝ 1/f. Current I = NBA×4π²f²C → I ∝ f².
  • DC source + inductor: Reactance ignored; current limited by ohmic resistance only.
  • Capacitor with DC: Blocks DC after fully charging (I = 0 at steady state).

The Rules

Hand rules, Lenz's law, and direction conventions

👍
Rule 01
Ampere's Right-Hand Rule
🎯 Used for: Direction of B from current
Straight wire: thumb → current direction, curled fingers → B direction. Coil/solenoid: thumb → B (North), curled fingers → current direction. Applies to straight wire, circular coil, solenoid.
🔩
Rule 02
Right-Hand Screw Rule
🎯 Used for: B direction in coils & magnetic dipole
Rotate screw clockwise in direction of current → screw advances in direction of B field. Also used for magnetic dipole moment direction. Can use in straight wire but inverse.
🔄
Rule 03
Clockwise/Anti-clockwise Rule
🎯 Used for: Polarity of coil face
Current clockwise when viewed from a face → that face is South pole. Current anti-clockwise when viewed from a face → that face is North pole.
🤚
Rule 04
Fleming's Left-Hand Rule
🎯 Used for: Force on current-carrying wire in B field
Left hand, three fingers perpendicular: Pointer → B field direction, Middle finger → conventional current direction, Thumb → Force direction. Also for: galvanometer deflection, motor rotation direction.
✋
Rule 05
Lenz's Rule
🎯 Used for: Direction of induced current in a COIL
Induced current always opposes the change in flux that created it. Increasing flux → induced current creates opposing flux. N pole approaching → coil becomes S (repels). N pole receding → coil becomes N (attracts). For straight wire: use Fleming's Right-Hand Rule instead.
🖐️
Rule 06
Fleming's Right-Hand Rule
🎯 Used for: Induced current in straight wire / dynamo
Right hand, three fingers perpendicular: Pointer → B field direction, Thumb → velocity (motion) direction, Middle finger → induced current direction. Wire acts as battery: current flows − to + inside. Also used for dynamo/motor coil direction.

When are values Zero or Maximum?

Common exam questions — conditions for extreme values

WHEN IS THE VALUE ZERO?
Voltage lost in a battery (Vᴮ − V)?
When no current flows (open circuit) OR when internal resistance r = 0.
Resultant B between two parallel wires?
When currents are in the same direction (neutral point between wires).
Resultant B at midpoint between two wires?
When currents are equal in magnitude and same direction.
Force on wire in magnetic field?
When wire is parallel to magnetic flux lines (θ = 0°, F = BIl·sin0° = 0).
Magnetic torque on coil in B field?
When coil is perpendicular to B (normal ∥ B, θ = 0°, τ = BIANsin0° = 0).
Induced emf in straight wire moving in B?
When wire moves parallel to B lines (θ = 0°) OR wire in open circuit.
Instantaneous emf in dynamo coil?
When coil is perpendicular to B (normal ∥ B, θ = 0°).
Back emf in inductive coil / AC current growth rate?
At the moment the current reaches its maximum value (ΔI/Δt = 0).
Phase angle in RLC circuit?
When X_L = X_C (resonance condition) → Z = R, θ = 0.
DC current in circuit with capacitor?
When capacitor is fully charged (V_capacitor = emf of battery).
Current in primary coil of transformer?
When secondary circuit is open (back emf = source emf, net = 0).
B in solenoid (double-wound coil)?
When double wounded — adjacent turns produce equal opposite fields that cancel.
WHEN IS THE VALUE MAXIMUM?
Potential difference between battery poles?
In open circuit (I = 0) OR when internal resistance r = 0.
Magnetic force on wire in B field?
When wire makes 90° with B (wire ⊥ B, F = BIl·sin90° = BIl).
Induced emf in straight wire?
When wire moves perpendicular to B (velocity ⊥ B, θ = 90°).
Back emf when closing switch on inductive coil?
At the moment of closing: back emf = full emf of source (I = 0 at that instant).
Magnetic torque / emf in dynamo?
When coil plane is parallel to B (normal ⊥ B, θ = 90°).
Photon frequency from excited hydrogen?
In Lyman series (electron falls from ∞ to n=1).
Photon wavelength from excited hydrogen?
In Pfund series (electron falls from n=6 to n=5).
Current in RLC circuit?
At resonance (f = f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)) when Z = R is minimum.
